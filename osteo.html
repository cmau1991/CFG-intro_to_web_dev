    What is Osteoporosis?

    Osteoporosis occurs when the loss of bone exceeds the creation of new bone.

    Osteoporosis causes bones to become weak and brittle. The condition can result in fractured bones from a minor fall or simple movements such as bending over or coughing.

    Osteoporosis-related breaks most commonly occur in the hip, wrist or spine.

    KEY TO NOTE: Bone is a living tissue that is constantly being broken down and replaced.

    Signs and Symptoms

    What warning signs should you be aware of?

    In the early stages of the disease, there usually no symptoms. However, once your bones are weakened by osteoporosis, you may experience:

  • Back pain - due to broken or collapsed bones in the spine.
  • Loss of height over time.
  • A stooped posture.
  • A bone that breaks much more easily than expected.

    • Diagnosis

    To diagnose Osteoporosis, your doctor may send you for:

    A DEXA scan measures bone density via a machine that uses low level X-rays to determine the composition of minerals in your bones.

    During the test, you lie on a padded table as a scanner passes over your body. Usually only the hip and spine are scanned.

    Treatment Options

    Treatment recommendations are often based on an estimate of your risk of breaking a bone in the next 10 years using DEXA scan results. Note: If you are not deemed high risk, treatment may focus on modifying risk factors for bone loss and falls.

  • Bisphosphonates.
  • Biological agents such as denosumab.
  • Hormome-related therapies.
  • Bone-building medicines for severe disease / where other therapies are failing:
  • Teriparatide
  • Abaloparatide
  • Romosozumab
